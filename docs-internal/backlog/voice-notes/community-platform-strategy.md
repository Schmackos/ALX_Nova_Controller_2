# Voice Note: Community Platform Strategy

## Summary
- Researching the best platform for building an ALX community
- Two main options: leverage an existing forum (DIY audio, ASR, etc.) vs. host a dedicated proprietary platform
- Target audience: end users, mezzanine creators, and companies
- Goal: brand-champion community members help each other resolve issues
- Issues flagged by the community get submitted through GitHub for the ALX platform team to address
- Purpose is maintaining contact, getting quick feedback, and enabling peer-to-peer support

## Cleaned Transcript

For community building — or creating a community, rather — research and give me an answer on what would be the best place to create a community for the ALX platform. Would it be leveraging a forum or community that already exists online, like a DIY audio forum or an ASR forum or something similar? Or would it be better to create a dedicated proprietary platform that we host, manage, and control?

The intention is to create a place where end users, mezzanine creators, and also companies can come together — where especially brand-champion community members help each other to resolve possible issues or flag issues that then get submitted through GitHub so they can be resolved by the ALX platform team. It's a way to stay in contact, get quick feedback, and have community members help each other.
